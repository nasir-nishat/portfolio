---
title: "How to ship an MVP without making a mess"
description: "Practical advice on getting your first version out the door without building technical debt you'll regret later."
date: "2026"
tag: "MVP"
---

Shipping fast is easy. Shipping something that doesn't fall apart once you have ten users is the hard part. I've seen too many projects get stuck in "refactoring hell" because they cut too many corners on day one.

My rule of thumb for MVPs:
- **Pick boring tech**: Unless your product *is* a new database, don't use a beta database. Stick to what you know.
- **Hard-code where it makes sense**: You don't need a complex CMS for your first landing page. Just write the HTML.
- **Focus on the core loop**: What's the one thing users *must* be able to do? Optimize that and ignore the rest.

The goal isn't to build a perfect product; it's to build a product that's good enough to tell you if you're on the right track.
